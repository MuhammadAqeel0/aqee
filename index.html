Bombay Biryani Recipe

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Basmati rice (soaked for 30 mins)
  • 500g chicken or mutton (cut into pieces)
  • 2 large onions (sliced)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 potatoes (peeled and halved)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 2-3 green chilies (slit)
  • A handful of mint leaves
  • A handful of coriander leaves
  • Whole spices (2 cloves, 2 cardamoms, 1 bay leaf, 1 small cinnamon stick)
  • 1/4 teaspoon saffron soaked in warm milk (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil or ghee as needed

Instructions

  1. Boil the basmati rice till it's 70% cooked. Drain and set aside.
  2. Fry potatoes till golden brown and set aside.
  3. Heat oil/ghee in a pot and add whole spices.
  4. Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  5. Add ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, and sauté briefly.
  6. Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric, red chili powder, and cook till tomatoes soften.
  7. Stir in yogurt, mint, coriander, and then add chicken or mutton. Cook until meat is tender.
  8. Layer the biryani: Add a layer of meat, a layer of rice, some fried potatoes, saffron milk, and mint/coriander. Repeat layers as needed.
  9. Cover and cook on low heat (dum) for 20-25 minutes.
  10. Gently mix before serving. Serve hot with raita or salad.

Enjoy your delicious homemade Bombay Biryani!
